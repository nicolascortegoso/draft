Your question involves a nuanced morphological analysis of the Yakut word "көнөтүк", and both interpretations you propose are plausible. Let's break them down systematically:

1. Analysis as көнө (Adj) + түк (ADVZ)

-түк as an adverb-forming suffix (cf. чэгиртүк "quickly," сэрэхтүк "carefully").

Interpretation: "in a straight manner" → "straightforwardly, directly."

Pros:

Fits attested adjective көнө.

Parallels other -түк adverbs derived from adjectives.

Cons:

Doesn’t account for the possible verbal root көн-.

2. Analysis as көн (V) + ө (CONV) + түк (ADVZ)

Interpretation: "straighteningly" → "directly, without deviation."

Pros:

Accounts for the verbal root, which may be primary.

Converbs often adverbialize naturally (e.g., туран "standing" → "immediately").

Cons:

Less transparent if көнө is already lexicalized as an adjective.
