The base form of the verb is the imperative of the second person singular.
All other verb forms are the result of affixation [Kharitonov, 1947:168].

Verbal roots can be of two types: primary and derived. Derived forms can be from verbal stems or from other types [Kharitonov, 1947:169]. 

Phonetic characteristic of verbal stems [Kharitonov, 1947:169]:
- in contrast to nominal stems, there are no narrow vowels *{ы, и, у, ү}* or single open *{а, э, о, ө}*.
- long open vowels such as *{аа, ээ, оо, өө}* are common, which at turn there seldom occur as nominal endings.
- monosyllabic stems often end in *{т, р, к, с}*.
- *{x}* that is fairly common in disyllabic and polisyllabic nominals, is rarely found in verbal stems.
